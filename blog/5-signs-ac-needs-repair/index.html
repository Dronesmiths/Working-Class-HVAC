1. Warm Air Blowing from Vents

This is the most obvious sign, but also one people try to ignore. If you put your hand over a register and the air feels room temperature or barely cool, something is wrong. It could be a refrigerant leak, a failing compressor, or even just a frozen coil. Regardless, it won't fix itself.

2. Weak Airflow

Is your fan running, but the air coming out of the vents feels like a whisper? Weak airflow often points to a clog in your ductwork, a dirty air filter, or a failing blower motor. If the air can't circulate effectively, your home will never reach the temperature you set on the thermostat.

3. Strange Noises

AC units should be relatively quietâ€”a steady hum is normal. Whatâ€™s not normal?

  • Grinding: Often a sign of motor bearing failure.
  • Squealing: Could indicate a loose belt.
  • Banging or Clanking: A loose part may be rattling around inside the unit.

Hearing any of these? Turn the system off and call a pro immediately before loose parts cause major damage.

4. Frequent Cycling

Does your AC turn on, run for a few minutes, turn off, and then turn right back on again? This is called "short cycling." It puts immense stress on your compressor and drives your energy bills through the roof. It can be caused by anything from a dirty filter to an oversized unit, but it always requires professional diagnosis.
