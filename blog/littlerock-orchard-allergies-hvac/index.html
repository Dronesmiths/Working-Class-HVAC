Breathing Near the Orchards: HVAC Tips for Littlerock

We wouldn't trade our fresh fruit for anything, but the pollen is intense. Keep your air clean.

Posted on August 28, 2025 | Location: Littlerock, CA

Littlerock is famous for its peaches, pears, and cherries. But along with those blossoms comes a massive amount of pollen. If your home isn't sealed properly, that pollen ends up on your pillows.

Seal Your Ducts

In older farmhouses, ductwork is often leaky. When the AC turns on, it sucks dust and pollen from the attic or crawlspace into the ducts and blows it into your living room. Sealing your ducts is the most effective way to improve indoor air quality.

Upgrade to Media Filters

A standard 1-inch filter clogs up in about 2 weeks during blossom season. We recommend installing a 4-inch "Media Filter Cabinet." These thick filters have way more surface area, meaning they trap more pollen without restricting airflow, and they last for 6 months.
