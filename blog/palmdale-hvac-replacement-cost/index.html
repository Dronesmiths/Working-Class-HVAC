HVAC Replacement Costs in Palmdale: What to Expect

From tract homes to custom builds, here is what a new system costs.

Posted on July 20, 2025 | Location: Palmdale, CA

Palmdale has a wide variety of housing stock, from the newer tract homes in Rancho Vista to the older ranch properties on the East side. Because systems rarely last more than 15-20 years in the desert, replacement is an evitable part of homeownership.

The "System Match" Factor

In Palmdale, we see two main types of installations:

  • Split Systems: Common in 2-story homes, with a condenser outside and a furnace in the attic or garage. Replacement typically ranges from $8,000 to $14,000 for a full system.
  • Package Units: Common on roofs of single-story homes. These are often easier to swap out (if the crane access is easy) and can range from $7,000 to $12,000.

SEER2 and Energy Efficiency

New California regulations (SEER2) mean that the absolute cheapest units you could buy 5 years ago are no longer legal to install. While modern units are more expensive upfront, they are significantly more efficient.

A new 14.3 SEER2 system can lower your Palmdale summer electric bill by 20-30% compared to an old 10 SEER unit.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

When getting quotes, make sure these are included:

  • Permits: Palmdale requires permits for change-outs. Make sure your contractor pulls them.
  • Ductwork transition: New units are often physically larger than old ones, requiring sheet metal work.
  • Crane fees: For rooftop units, the crane rental is usually $300-$500 depending on the reach.
