What is a Heat Pump?

A heat pump is an all-in-one system that heats and cools your home. In the summer, it works exactly like a standard air conditioner. In the winter, it reverses the process, pulling heat from the outside air to warm your home.

Because they move heat rather than creating it by burning gas, heat pumps are incredibly efficient.

Why Choose a Heat Pump in the Antelope Valley?

  • Solar Friendly: If you have solar panels, a heat pump allows you to heat your home using your own generated power instead of expensive natural gas.
  • Dual-Fuel Capability: We can install a "Hybrid" system that uses a heat pump for mild winter days (40-60°F) and switches to a gas furnace only for the freezing nights.
  • Safety: No gas lines means no risk of carbon monoxide leaks from the heat pump unit itself.

Common Heat Pump Issue: "It's Smoking!"

In the winter, you might see steam rising from your outdoor unit. This is normal! It's called Defrost Mode. The unit is melting ice off its coils to keep running efficiently. Read more about Defrost Mode here.
